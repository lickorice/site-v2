Module 3

This assessment helped me approach speeches as less of an audience, but more of a critic. I had to point out persuasive strategies Phoenix used in his Oscar acceptance speech.

Being a little more skeptical about everything isn't always harmful. Most times it draws the line between gullible and attentive. Too much skepticism may be viewed as rude, but for this assessment, I guess Joaquin Phoenix doesn't really mind.

Personally, I liked his speech. It felt like from the heart, in an audience standpoint. From the Speech 30 standpoint, it really does exhibit good use persuasive strategies. I'm now much more aware in (mentally) pointing out these in most speeches I listen to now!

Module 4

Again, another mini-critique of one speech (of many!) by Rep. AOC about discrimination. Nothing really new here, but it helped me classify speeches easier via the classical classifications (mini-alliteration not intended) of rhetoric.

Much like the earlier examples and more to come, this mainly helped me realize the presence of the topic at hand in everyday situations. In this case, it was the three types of classical rhetoric: deliberative, judicial, and ceremonial.

TL;DR: I believe her speech was mostly deliberative/legislative. Which is appropriate, since she's in Congress.
